# Podcasts & Voices

Curated audio sources the founder wants in the mix. **Read this honestly:** unlike the book
canon, a podcast's specific lessons live in individual episodes, not in a title. Distilling
"principles" for a show from general reputation would be fabrication — the exact failure mode
this skill avoids. So this file does two things: (1) accurately characterizes each source and
what it's *for*, and (2) holds a slot for real, episode-level lessons to be added over time.

**How to add real lessons (keep it faithful):** after listening to an episode (or pasting a
transcript/notes), distill it to the same format used elsewhere — *source → 2–4 applicable
principles → "Apply to:"* — and drop it under the right show below. Attribute the episode and
guest. Never invent a takeaway you didn't actually hear.

## La Fábrica 🇦🇷
**What it is:** an Argentine business/industry podcast that became one of the country's most
listened-to shows, telling the real stories of industrialists, entrepreneurs, and builders
across industry, agro, real estate, gastronomía, finance, and more — "putting a voice to
those who build in silence."

**Why it earns a place here:** it's a *counterweight to the US-tech-centric canon*. Most of
the books above assume Silicon Valley conditions (deep capital, huge markets, software
margins). La Fábrica is founders building in a hard macro — inflation, scarce financing,
regulation, the real economy of an emerging market. For a Spanish-speaking founder, that
context often maps better than a16z's playbook. Reach for its lessons when the decision is
about operating under constraint, real-economy/industrial businesses, or the LatAm context.

## La Cruda 🎙️
**What it is:** an intimate, unfiltered interview podcast by Migue Granados (with Rodrigo
Espector) — *not* an entrepreneurship show. Its premise is to "ask and argue down to the last
vertebra," to get people to drop the mask and talk about what's really going on.

**Why it's here — as method, not content:** don't mine it for business principles; it has
none to give. Its value to *this* skill is that it models the exact **posture** the copilot is
supposed to embody — push past the polished answer until the real thing surfaces, refuse the
comfortable pretense. When you're stress-testing a founder's reasoning, that's the register:
warm but relentless, digging for the truth under the story they tell themselves. Treat La
Cruda as a reminder of *how to interrogate*, not *what to conclude*.
