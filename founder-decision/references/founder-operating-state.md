# The Founder's Operating State

*Grounded in the science popularized by Andrew Huberman (Huberman Lab) — sleep, stress,
focus, dopamine, and circadian biology.*

The premise the rest of this skill quietly depends on: **the founder is the company's
decision-making instrument, and a dysregulated instrument makes bad calls.** You can run a
flawless decision process on top of a sleep-deprived, stressed, dopamine-crashed brain and
still decide badly, because the hardware itself is degraded. Managing your own state isn't
self-care fluff — it's a strategic input to decision quality, and its neglect is upstream of
the "founder burnout / disharmony" failure mode that kills companies before the business does.

*This is general performance and wellbeing framing, not medical advice.*

## When to actually use this
Bring this in when: a big **irreversible** decision is on the table (check the operator
before pulling the trigger), the founder sounds fried/reactive, the decision is being made
in an emotional spike, or the question is really about pace and sustainability rather than a
single call.

## The core principles

- **Sleep is the foundation of judgment.** Sleep loss degrades exactly the faculties a
  founder decides with — prefrontal risk assessment, impulse control, emotional regulation.
  A one-way-door decision made badly under-slept is systematically worse. The move is almost
  always: *sleep on it, decide tomorrow.* If someone is about to make a permanent call while
  exhausted, that alone is a reason to wait.

- **Regulate state before deciding (HALT + stress).** Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired — and
  acutely stressed. Acute stress narrows cognition into fight-or-flight; you literally lose
  the ability to see options and second-order effects. This is the biological mechanism
  behind "attain distance before deciding." Down-regulate first (a physiological sigh, a
  walk, a night's sleep), *then* run the decision. A decision made mid-spike is the emotion
  deciding, not you.

- **Stress is a tool, not only a threat.** Short-term stress sharpens focus and performance;
  it's *chronic, unrecovered* stress that corrodes health and judgment. The skill isn't
  avoiding stress — it's deploying it in bursts and then deliberately discharging it
  (recovery, sleep, movement). A founder running permanently redlined will make progressively
  worse calls and eventually break.

- **Manage dopamine baseline, don't chase peaks.** Motivation runs on dopamine, but chasing
  constant highs (hype, launches, wins) spikes then crashes it, and decisions made in the
  post-peak trough skew pessimistic and rash. Sustainable drive comes from protecting the
  *baseline* — which means not tying your sense of the venture to the last dopamine hit, and
  not making strategic calls in a crash.

- **Protect your peak cognitive window.** Focus runs in ~90-minute ultradian bouts, and most
  people have a daily peak (often morning). Do the hardest thinking — including your biggest
  decisions — in that window, not at your daily low or at 11pm. *When* in the day you decide
  changes the quality of the decision.

- **Circadian anchors are the substrate of long-run judgment.** Morning light, regular
  movement, consistent meal and sleep timing stabilize mood and cognition. What actually
  builds a company is *consistent* good judgment sustained over years — and that consistency
  is downstream of a stable operating rhythm, not heroic all-nighters.

**Apply to:** whether to make or *defer* a big decision, decision timing (within the day and
week), sustainable pace vs. burnout, and any moment where the founder's state — not the
facts — seems to be driving the call.
